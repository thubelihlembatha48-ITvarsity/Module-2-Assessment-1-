Elephants

Elephants are large mammals of the family of Elephantidae and the order proboscidea. Two species are traditionally recorgnised, the African Elephant (Loxodonta Africans) and the Asians elephant (Elephas Maiximus). Elephants are scattered through Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia and South East Asia. Male African Elephants are the largest extent terrestrial animals and can reach a height of 4m (13ft) and weigh 7 000 kg (15 000 lb).

All elephants have several distinctive features the most notable of which is a long trunk and proboscis, used for many purposes, particularly breathing, lifting water and grasping objects. Their incisors grow into tusks which can severe as weapons and as tools for moving objects and digging. Elephants large ears flaps help to control their body temperature. Their pillar-like legs can carry their great weight. African elephants have larger ears and concaved backs while Asians elephants have smaller ears and convex or level backs.

Elephants are herbivorous and can be found in different habitats including savannas, forests, deserts and marshes. They prefer to stay near water.
